# CLINICAL TRIAL PROTOCOL TABLE - COMPLETED

Based on EudraCT 13071 and BEtreat Study (EudraCT 2016-002808-19) as reference studies.

## STUDY PARAMETERS

| **Section** | **Details** |
|---|---|
| **Title** | Phase III Randomised, Double-blind, Placebo-controlled Study to Evaluate the Non-Inferior Efficacy of [IMP Formulation] versus [Active Comparator] in Uncomplicated Vulvovaginal Candidosis (VVC) |
| **Centres** | 15-20 investigational sites in EEA countries (Germany, Bulgaria, Italy, Poland, Russian Federation) |
| **Main Objective** | To demonstrate non-inferior efficacy of [Test IMP] versus [Active Comparator] in terms of overall response (clinical cure and mycological cure) 10-14 days post-application in uncomplicated VVC |

## TRIAL DESIGN

| **Parameter** | **Specification** |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Phase III, randomised, double-blind, parallel-group, placebo-controlled trial |
| **Type** | Therapeutic confirmatory (Phase III) |
| **Blinding** | Double-blind (investigator and subject blinded to treatment allocation) |
| **Control Type** | Placebo control + Active comparator |
| **Randomisation** | 1:1:1 ratio (Test IMP : Active Comparator : Placebo) |
| **Duration** | Approximately 9 months recruitment + 60 days follow-up per subject |

## POPULATION

| **Section** | **Criteria** |
|---|---|
| **Target Population** | Adult females (18-65 years) with uncomplicated vulvovaginal candidosis |
| **Sample Composition** | 18-64 years: primary population; 65+ years: permitted with consideration |

## SELECTION CRITERIA

### **Inclusion**
1. Non-pregnant females aged 18-65 years (inclusive)
2. Clinically diagnosed uncomplicated VVC confirmed by:
   - Positive vaginal wet mount test (microscopy)
   - Mycological culture or rapid detection methods
   - Baseline symptoms: Total symptom score ≥3 points
   - Baseline signs: Total signs score ≥1 point
3. Symptomatic presentation including: itching, burning, irritation, vaginal discharge, dysuria
4. Clinical signs: vaginal edema, erythema, excoriation, vulval edema, erythema
5. Negative Trichomonas vaginalis saline smear
6. Females of childbearing potential:
   - Negative pregnancy test at screening
   - Agreement to use acceptable contraception (combined hormonal contraceptives, progestin-only methods, IUD/IUS, bilateral tubal occlusion, vasectomised partner, sexual abstinence)
7. Women of postmenopausal state (no menses ≥12 months)
8. Able to provide informed consent and cooperate with trial procedures

### **Exclusion**
1. Known hypersensitivity to imidazoles, triazoles, or trial medication components
2. Pregnancy, lactation, or breast-feeding
3. Bacterial vaginosis or concurrent infections:
   - Trichomonas vaginalis
   - Chlamydia trachomatis
   - Neisseria gonorrhoeae
   - Herpes simplex virus
   - Human papillomavirus
4. Vaginitis of different etiology (protozoan infections confirmed by microscopy)
5. Signs/symptoms suggesting complications:
   - Abdominal pain, fever
   - Foul-smelling vaginal discharge
   - Irregular or abnormal vaginal bleeding
6. Gynecological conditions interfering with trial assessment:
   - Genital tract abnormalities
   - Lichen sclerosus
   - Post-operative genital tract alterations
   - Genital neoplasm
7. Antimycotic treatment history:
   - Systemic antifungal therapy within 4 weeks pre-screening
   - Topical antifungal therapy within 1 week pre-screening
   - Intravaginal/systemic antimycotic within 60 days pre-screening
8. Concurrent infections predisposing to mycosis:
   - Uncontrolled diabetes mellitus
   - Immunocompromised patients
   - HIV positive status (if inclusion/exclusion determination required)
9. Chronic/recurrent vulvovaginal mycosis (≥4 mycologically proven episodes in 12 months)
10. Immunosuppression therapy:
    - Systemic corticosteroids
    - Immunosuppressive agents
    - Anti-infectives affecting immunity
11. Concomitant medications:
    - Topical drugs on application area (within 2 weeks)
    - Systemic anticoagulants (warfarin, acenocoumarol)
    - Anti-inflammatory/analgesic drugs
    - Antibiotics/antibacterials
    - Contraceptive foams, creams, jellies, sponges
    - Therapeutic ointments, condoms, diaphragms
    - OTC vaginal products
12. Vaginal tampon use during treatment and 3 days post-treatment
13. Feminine hygiene products (douches, deodorants) for 2 weeks from Visit 1-2
14. Sexual activity abstinence not feasible during 3 days post-treatment
15. Menstruation at screening or expected within 4 days post-screening
16. Investigational drug within 30 days pre-screening
17. Clinically significant abnormalities:
    - Physical examination findings
    - Vital signs abnormalities
    - ECG abnormalities
    - Laboratory test abnormalities
18. Vulnerable populations (detained/incarcerated individuals)
19. Study site personnel involvement

## PRIMARY ENDPOINTS

| **Endpoint** | **Definition** | **Timing** | **Analysis** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Overall Response (Co-primary)** | Clinical cure + mycological cure | Visit 2: 10-14 days post-application | Responder rate (%) with 95% CI; Per-Protocol population |
| **Mycological Cure (Co-primary)** | Absence of Candida/yeasts at microscopy; Negative mycological culture | Visit 2 (10-14 days) & Visit 3 (6-8 weeks) | Percentage of subjects (%) with 95% CI |
| **Clinical Cure (Co-primary)** | Absence of symptoms (itching, burning, irritation) + resolution of signs (edema, erythema, excoriation) | Visit 2 (10-14 days) & Visit 3 (6-8 weeks) | Clinical symptoms & signs assessed separately |

## SECONDARY ENDPOINTS

1. **Mycological Cure** at Week 6-8 post-application
2. **Clinical Cure** (symptoms resolution) at 6-8 weeks
3. **Clinical Improvement** (≥50% reduction of signs/symptoms)
4. **Clinical Failure** (no improvement or worsening)
5. **Time to First Symptom Relief** (reduction ≥1 point in total symptom score)
6. **Time to 50% Symptom Reduction** (post-hoc analysis)
7. **Time to Complete Symptom Resolution** (total score = 0)
8. **Therapeutic Cure** (combined mycological + clinical cure at Visit 4)
9. **Recurrence Rate** at Follow-up (presence of Candida in previously cured subjects)
10. **Acceptability Assessment**:
    - Comfort of formulation
    - Stickiness/greasiness
    - Staining properties
    - Vaginal discharge/flowing out
    - Dissolution characteristics
11. **Safety and Tolerability**:
    - Adverse event profile (number, severity, causality)
    - Serious adverse events reporting
    - Local and systemic tolerability

## NON-INFERIORITY CRITERION

| **Specification** | **Details** |
|---|---|
| **Non-Inferiority Margin** | -10.0 percentage points for mycological/clinical cure rates |
| **Confidence Level** | 95% two-sided confidence interval |
| **Definition** | Test IMP non-inferior to active comparator if lower 95% CI of difference ≥ -10.0 percentage points |
| **Statistical Method** | Fisher's exact test; 95% CI for treatment difference |
| **Hypothesis** | H0: Test IMP inferior to active comparator; H1: Test IMP non-inferior to active comparator |
