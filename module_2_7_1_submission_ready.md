# 2.7.1 Summary of Biopharmaceutic Studies and Associated Analytical Methods

**Medicinal product:** Diclofenac sodium 50 mg/g gel  
**Legal basis:** Hybrid application under Article 10(3) of Directive 2001/83/EC  
**Reference medicinal product:** Diflex 50 mg/g gel (Fiterman Pharma SRL, Romania)

## 2.7.1.1 Background and Overview

Diclofenac sodium 50 mg/g gel is a locally applied, locally acting topical semisolid intended for the symptomatic local treatment of pain, inflammation and oedema associated with mild to moderate muscle pain and post-traumatic conditions involving tendons, ligaments, muscles and joints.[file:30][file:31][file:32]

This application is submitted as a hybrid application under Article 10(3) of Directive 2001/83/EC, with Diflex 50 mg/g gel identified as the reference medicinal product in the dossier.[file:31][file:32]

For locally applied topical products of this type, the key biopharmaceutic question is whether the test product demonstrates comparable release performance to the reference product under controlled in vitro conditions. The submitted biopharmaceutic evidence therefore consists of a comparative IVRT study designed to assess release from the dosage form into a receptor medium across an inert artificial membrane.[file:30][file:31]

The dossier also describes the product as a simple formulation in which the active substance is fully solubilized in the gel matrix. On this basis, the IVRT approach is used as the relevant biopharmaceutic performance test to support the application.[file:30][file:31]

## 2.7.1.2 Summary of Results of Individual Studies

### Study 1: Comparative in vitro release testing of Diclofenac sodium 50 mg/g gels

A comparative IVRT study was performed by the Laboratory of Biopharmaceutics, Faculty of Pharmacy, Carol Davila University of Medicine and Pharmacy, Bucharest, and reported in the final study report dated 25 February 2021.[file:30]

The study compared four topical gel products containing diclofenac sodium 50 mg/g. In the study report, the applicant’s product was identified as Product B, while Product A corresponded to Diflex 50 mg/g gel, Product C to Clafen 50 mg/g gel, and Product D to Diclac 50 mg/g gel.[file:30]

The study objective was to compare the in vitro release profiles of diclofenac sodium and to assess whether observed release similarity could be considered predictive of comparable in vivo performance for these semisolid formulations.[file:30]

The test was conducted using a Hanson Microette static vertical diffusion cell system under occluded, pseudo-infinite dose conditions. Approximately 300 mg of product was applied directly from the primary package onto a hydrophilic artificial polysulfone membrane (25 mm diameter, 0.45 μm pore size), after pre-soaking of the membrane in receptor medium.[file:30]

The receptor medium was a hydroalcoholic mixture containing 30% absolute ethanol in water, selected to maintain sink conditions for diclofenac sodium. The system was maintained at 32°C and stirred at 600 rpm throughout the experiment.[file:30]

Sampling was performed at 15, 30, 45, 60, 90, 120, 180, 240, 300 and 360 minutes. Samples were collected manually and replaced with fresh receptor medium as part of the closed-system design.[file:30]

Diclofenac sodium in the receptor medium was quantified by a validated HPLC-UV method using an Agilent 1200 chromatographic system. The column used was ODS Hypersil, 150 × 4 mm, 5 μm. The mobile phase consisted of acetonitrile:methanol:10 mM phosphate buffer pH 3.3 in a 40:40:20 ratio, with isocratic elution at 1.0 mL/min, column temperature 40°C, and UV detection at 278 nm.[file:30]

The analytical method was reported as linear over the range 0.2 to 200 μg/mL, with calibration and quality control samples prepared at 3, 30 and 120 μg/mL using sponsor-provided diclofenac sodium working standard.[file:30]

The release rate was calculated from the slope of cumulative amount released per unit area versus the square root of time according to the Higuchi model. Similarity was assessed using the rank-order slope-ratio approach described in SUPAC-SS/USP-style IVRT methodology, with a 90% confidence interval acceptance interval of 75.00% to 133.33%.[file:30][file:31]

According to the attached clinical summary, comparison of the applicant’s product against Diflex 50 mg/g gel yielded 90% confidence intervals of 94.64% to 107.99% for the 0–180 minute interval and 92.55% to 112.78% for the 0–360 minute interval.[file:31]

Both intervals are within the predefined acceptance limits and therefore support in vitro release similarity between the applicant’s product and the reference medicinal product under the conditions of the study.[file:31]

The IVRT report summary also states that the applicant’s product showed similarity versus all three comparator products, with the highest similarity versus Diflex 50 mg/g gel.[file:30]

## 2.7.1.3 Comparison and Analyses of Results Across Studies

Only one dedicated biopharmaceutic study is submitted for this application, namely the comparative IVRT study described above.[file:30][file:31]

Accordingly, no cross-study comparison, pooling, or meta-analysis is applicable to section 2.7.1.3. The relevant comparison is the within-study comparison between the applicant’s product and the reference medicinal product Diflex 50 mg/g gel, which demonstrated in vitro release similarity within the predefined acceptance criteria.[file:30][file:31]

The formulation comparison in the dossier supports the relevance of this result, as the applicant’s product and the reference product are described as highly similar in qualitative and quantitative composition, dosage form, route of administration, target population and intended use.[file:30]

On this basis, the IVRT findings provide the principal biopharmaceutic support for the application and do not indicate a formulation-related difference expected to alter dosage-form release performance in a clinically meaningful way.[file:30][file:31][file:32]

## Overall conclusion

The submitted biopharmaceutic evidence for diclofenac sodium 50 mg/g gel consists of a single comparative IVRT study performed under controlled conditions using a validated analytical method.[file:30]

The study demonstrated in vitro release similarity between the applicant’s product and the reference medicinal product Diflex 50 mg/g gel, with reported 90% confidence intervals within the predefined acceptance limits.[file:31]
